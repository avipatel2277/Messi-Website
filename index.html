He was born in Rosario, Argentina and lived with his parents and siblings. He started playing football at an early age and joined his local football club, Newell's Old Boys at the age of 7 (in 1995). At the age of 11, he was diagnosed with a growth hormone deficiency. It is a medical condition in which the body does not produce enough growth hormones. The treatment for this was very expensive and his family was not able to afford it. This caused Messi's growth to stagnate at an early age, making him considerably smaller than other kids in his age group. However, Messi did not give up and he continued to play football. His incredible footballing talent was spotted at an early age by FC Barcelona. They offered to help Messi and his family to move to Barcelona and pay for Messi's growth-hormone deficiency treatment if Messi would join their youth setup. With Barca's help, Messi was able to recover most of his growth. He went on to play for their youth ranks and make his debut in the 2004-05 season. He broke the La Liga record for the youngest footballer to play a league game, and he also broke the record as the youngest player to score a goal in La Liga. After this, his Argentina senior team debut followed in 2005. Major individual and team honours soon followed as he became an integral part of FC Barcelona's and Argentina's first team for many years to come. He won everything there was to win at Barcelona. And after 17 wonderful years in their senior squad, he finally moved onto a new chapter in his career. He left his boyhood club as he was unable to reach a contract extension with them and joined Ligue-1 giants PSG. Now at PSG, even at the twilight of his career, his hunger for trophies and success has not faded away and he continues to break records and post outstanding numbers. Messi will represent Argentina in the World Cup and it may well be his last chance to win the only trophy that has eluded him in his decorative career.
